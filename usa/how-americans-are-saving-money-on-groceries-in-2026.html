How Americans Are Saving Money on Groceries in 2026

Food prices are still high in 2026, but many Americans are finding smart ways to spend less while still eating healthy and feeding their families.

Groceries continue to be one of the biggest monthly expenses for families across America. Rising prices for meat, eggs, milk, snacks, and household products have forced many people to change the way they shop.

In 2026, smart grocery shopping is no longer just about finding the cheapest store. It is about planning ahead, using technology, and avoiding waste. Here are the biggest ways Americans are saving money on groceries this year.

Buying Store Brands Instead of Name Brands

One of the easiest ways to save money is by buying store brands. Many store-brand products taste almost the same as expensive national brands but cost much less.

People are switching to store-brand cereal, bread, pasta, frozen food, snacks, milk, and cleaning supplies to cut their grocery bills.

Money Tip: Store brands can often save shoppers 20% to 40% compared to popular name brands.

Using Cashback and Coupon Apps

Many shoppers now use apps to earn cashback or find coupons before they go to the store. Apps can help people save money on groceries, household items, and even gas.

Popular cashback and coupon apps include store apps, digital coupons, rewards programs, and rebate apps.

  • Digital store coupons
  • Cashback rewards apps
  • Loyalty card discounts
  • Weekly grocery promotions

Meal Planning for the Week

Many Americans are now planning meals before they go shopping. This helps them buy only what they need and avoid waste.

Meal planning can reduce impulse purchases and make it easier to stick to a budget. Families often create a weekly menu and build a shopping list around it.

Buying in Bulk

Buying in bulk can save a lot of money over time, especially for items used regularly.

Rice, pasta, beans, oats, canned food, frozen vegetables, toilet paper, and cleaning products are some of the most common bulk purchases.

However, bulk shopping only works if you use the items before they expire.

Shopping at Discount Stores

More people are shopping at discount grocery stores because prices are often lower than traditional supermarkets.

Discount stores may offer lower-cost produce, store-brand products, frozen foods, and meat at reduced prices.

Some shoppers even visit multiple stores to get the best deals on different items.

Buying Cheap High-Protein Foods

Protein is often one of the most expensive parts of a grocery bill. To save money, many people are choosing cheaper protein options.

  • Eggs
  • Beans
  • Peanut butter
  • Greek yogurt
  • Canned tuna
  • Chicken thighs
  • Lentils

These foods are affordable, filling, and can be used in many different meals.

Budget Tip: Buying cheap protein foods can lower your grocery bill while still helping you stay healthy and full.

Avoiding Food Waste

Food waste costs families a lot of money. Many people throw away fruits, vegetables, leftovers, and expired items every week.

Americans are now trying to use leftovers more often, freeze extra food, and buy only what they know they will eat.

Simple habits like checking the refrigerator before shopping can help avoid buying duplicate items.

Shopping on the Best Days

Some stores have better discounts on certain days of the week. Many shoppers look for sales when stores restock shelves or mark down fresh items.

Early morning shopping can also help people find better produce and reduced-price items before they sell out.

Using Warehouse Clubs

Warehouse clubs are becoming more popular because they allow people to buy large quantities at lower prices.

Families often use warehouse clubs for meat, drinks, snacks, paper products, and frozen food.

Sharing bulk purchases with friends or relatives can make these memberships even more valuable.

Budget Meal Prep Ideas

Meal prepping is another popular way Americans are saving money. Cooking meals at home is much cheaper than eating out or ordering delivery.

Popular meal prep ideas include:

  • Rice and chicken bowls
  • Pasta with vegetables
  • Bean and rice meals
  • Egg sandwiches
  • Overnight oats
  • Soup and bread

Preparing meals ahead of time can save both money and time during the week.

Conclusion

Grocery prices may still be high in 2026, but there are many smart ways to reduce costs. Buying store brands, planning meals, using cashback apps, and avoiding food waste can make a big difference.

Small changes in shopping habits can save hundreds or even thousands of dollars over a year. The key is staying organized and focusing on value instead of impulse purchases.
