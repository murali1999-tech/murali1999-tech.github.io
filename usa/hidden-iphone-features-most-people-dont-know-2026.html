Hidden iPhone Features Most People Still Don't Know About in 2026

Your iPhone has many hidden features that can save time, improve privacy, boost battery life, and make daily tasks much easier.

Most people use only a small part of what their iPhone can actually do. Apple keeps adding new features every year, but many of them stay hidden in the settings menu.

If you know these tricks, your iPhone can become faster, smarter, and easier to use. Here are some hidden iPhone features most people still do not know about in 2026.

Back Tap Shortcuts

One of the most useful hidden features is Back Tap. This allows you to tap the back of your iPhone two or three times to trigger actions.

You can set Back Tap to take screenshots, turn on the flashlight, open the camera, launch an app, or even activate Siri.

To enable it, go to Settings, Accessibility, Touch, then Back Tap.

Quick Tip: Set double tap for screenshots and triple tap for flashlight access. It can save a lot of time every day.

Hidden Photo Album Lock

Many people do not know that iPhone can lock hidden photos behind Face ID or passcode.

You can move private pictures into the hidden album and then require Face ID to view them. This gives extra privacy if someone else uses your phone.

Go to Photos, select the image, tap hide, and then enable Face ID protection in Settings.

Live Voicemail

Live Voicemail lets you see a real-time transcription of what a caller is saying before you decide whether to answer.

This is useful for unknown numbers, spam calls, or situations where you are busy but still want to know why someone is calling.

NameDrop

NameDrop makes sharing contact information easier than ever. You simply hold two iPhones close together and instantly exchange contact cards.

This is much faster than typing phone numbers manually and is especially useful at events, meetings, or parties.

Visual Look Up

Visual Look Up can identify plants, pets, landmarks, food, and many other objects directly from your photos.

Take a photo, swipe up on the image, and your iPhone may show information about what is in the picture.

This feature is useful for travel, gardening, food, and learning about animals.

Safari Tricks

Safari has many hidden tools that can make browsing easier. You can group tabs, hide distracting ads, save pages for offline reading, and automatically fill passwords.

Safari also has a Reader Mode that removes unnecessary ads and makes articles easier to read.

  • Use Reader Mode for cleaner articles
  • Group tabs by topic
  • Save pages offline
  • Use private browsing for privacy

Battery Saving Settings

Battery life is one of the biggest concerns for iPhone users. Fortunately, there are hidden settings that can help your battery last longer.

Turn off background app refresh, lower screen brightness, use dark mode, and enable Low Power Mode when needed.

You can also check which apps are using the most battery by going to Settings and Battery.

Battery Tip: Turning off background app refresh for social media apps can improve battery life significantly.

Secret Keyboard Shortcuts

The iPhone keyboard includes several shortcuts that most users never notice.

For example, holding down the space bar turns the keyboard into a trackpad, making it easier to move the cursor.

You can also hold letters to access special symbols, quickly undo typing by shaking the phone, and create text shortcuts for phrases you use often.

Hidden Camera Features

The iPhone camera has many advanced tools that people rarely use. You can scan documents, copy text from images, adjust focus, and use burst mode for action shots.

Portrait mode, cinematic mode, and night mode can also improve photo quality without extra apps.

If you press and hold the shutter button, you can record video instantly without switching modes.

Emergency SOS Features

Your iPhone can help protect you during emergencies. Emergency SOS can quickly call emergency services and send your location to trusted contacts.

You can activate it by pressing the side button and volume button together.

Medical ID is another useful feature because it lets emergency responders see important health information even if your phone is locked.

Conclusion

Your iPhone is more powerful than most people realize. Small hidden features like Back Tap, Live Voicemail, battery-saving settings, and secret keyboard shortcuts can make a big difference in everyday life.

The more you explore your iPhone settings, the more useful your device becomes. Many of these features only take a few minutes to set up but can save hours over time.
