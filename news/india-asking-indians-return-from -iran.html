Government of India Advisory – Indians in Iran

Introduction

In response to the evolving security situation in Iran, the Government of India has issued repeated advisories urging Indian nationals residing in or visiting Iran to return home or take all safety precautions. These advisories come amid escalating geopolitical tensions and uncertainties in the region.

India's Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) and the Embassy of India in Tehran have been actively monitoring the situation and communicating with Indian citizens through updates, advisory notices, and evacuation support efforts.

Government Advisory Details

As early as January this year, the MEA advised Indian nationals in Iran — including students, tourists, businesspeople, and residents — to leave the country by any available means of transport, including commercial flights, if possible.

The Embassy further instructed citizens to avoid areas of protests, stay in close contact with the Embassy, and keep important documents like passports ready in case evacuation became necessary.

These advisories reflect the Indian government’s emphasis on citizen safety and the unpredictable nature of regional security dynamics.

Recent Safety Instructions

In early April, with tensions intensifying in West Asia, the Embassy of India in Tehran issued a new advisory recommending that Indians “stay where they are for the next 48 hours” and avoid venturing outside unless absolutely necessary. This was primarily due to rapidly changing security conditions and fears of escalation.

The advisory emphasized that individuals should remain indoors, avoid electric infrastructure, military installations, and upper floors of large buildings, and follow instructions from local authorities closely.

Registering with the Embassy

The government strongly advised all Indian nationals currently in Iran to register their presence with the Embassy in Tehran if they had not done so already. Registration helps the Embassy share real‑time updates and organize evacuation support when required.

Emergency helpline numbers were also publicized, ensuring a direct line of communication for urgent assistance or travel coordination.

Evacuation Efforts & Return to India

The Government of India has been facilitating safe passage for Indian citizens willing to return. According to the MEA, over 1,777 Indian nationals have already been helped to exit Iran and return home through land borders with Armenia and Azerbaijan.

These evacuations included a mix of students, professionals, and fishermen employed by Iranian companies who were safely transported through neighboring countries before flying back home.

The Embassy remains in regular contact with those who have not yet left, offering guidance based on ground conditions and available travel routes.

Government Priorities and Citizen Safety

Throughout this unfolding situation, the Government of India has underscored its primary priority as the safety and well‑being of Indian citizens abroad. The MEA continuously reviews the security environment and updates advisories accordingly.

Keeping Indian nationals informed, facilitating their safe exit, and coordinating with international partners reflect India’s proactive approach to crisis response in foreign lands.

What Indian Nationals Should Do

  • Keep passport and identity documents readily accessible.
  • Register with the Indian Embassy in Tehran if not already done.
  • Monitor official advisories and updates regularly.
  • Avoid non‑essential travel and public gatherings.
  • Prepare to leave Iran by available transport if conditions worsen.

Staying informed and maintaining communication with the Embassy enhances the safety of individuals during rapidly changing situations.

Conclusion

In the face of an uncertain security landscape in Iran, the Government of India has repeatedly urged its citizens to leave the country and has supported many in doing so already. Through advisories, evacuation coordination, and safety guidance, the Indian government continues working to protect its nationals in Iran until the situation stabilizes and safe travel routes are fully restored.
