Delhi CM Assures No Disruption in LPG and Fuel Supply Across the City

Government says LPG cylinders, petrol, diesel, and other fuel supplies remain stable in Delhi.

The Delhi government has assured citizens that there will be no shortage or disruption in the supply of LPG cylinders, petrol, diesel, and other essential fuels across the city. The announcement came after rumors began spreading about possible shortages due to transport issues and supply chain concerns.

Delhi Chief Minister stated that residents do not need to panic or rush to buy extra fuel or gas cylinders. According to the government, all major fuel depots, LPG distributors, and supply chains are functioning normally.

Delhi government says there is enough LPG, petrol, and diesel stock available for regular use across the city.

Officials have clarified that oil companies and LPG agencies are continuing their operations without interruption. Tankers carrying fuel are entering the city regularly, and gas cylinder deliveries are also taking place as usual.

Authorities have urged the public not to believe false messages circulating on social media about fuel shortages. In recent days, several rumors claimed that LPG cylinders and petrol would become unavailable due to disruptions in transport routes. However, officials have said these claims are not true.

The government also warned that panic buying could create unnecessary pressure on the supply system. When people buy more than they need, it can cause temporary shortages in some areas even when the overall supply remains sufficient.

How LPG Supply Works in Delhi

Delhi depends heavily on regular LPG deliveries for household cooking needs. Thousands of gas cylinders are supplied daily through a network of distributors, warehouses, and transport vehicles.

Major public sector companies handle LPG distribution across the city. These agencies have confirmed that there are enough reserves available to meet customer demand. Booking systems are also functioning normally, and customers can continue to order cylinders through apps, websites, phone calls, or local agencies.

Officials said that no delivery delays have been reported on a large scale. In some cases, there may be minor delays due to traffic or local issues, but there is no city-wide crisis.

Citizens have been advised to continue their normal LPG booking routine and avoid storing extra cylinders unnecessarily.

Petrol and Diesel Supply Also Stable

Petrol pumps across Delhi are also operating normally. Long queues have not been reported in most areas, and stations are receiving fresh stock regularly. Oil companies have said that they are monitoring the situation closely and coordinating with local authorities.

Transport officials have ensured that fuel tankers can move freely across major roads and highways connected to Delhi. This has helped maintain a steady supply of petrol and diesel to fuel stations.

Commercial transport operators, delivery services, taxi drivers, and private vehicle owners have all been advised not to panic. Officials say there is no reason to expect any immediate shortage in fuel availability.

Government Response to Rumors

The Delhi government has taken steps to monitor social media and stop the spread of false information. Authorities believe that misleading messages can create confusion and unnecessary fear among the public.

Officials have urged people to rely only on official government announcements and trusted news sources for updates. They also encouraged residents to report fake messages that claim fuel or LPG supplies are ending.

In previous situations, rumors about shortages have led to panic buying, crowded petrol pumps, and long waiting times for gas cylinders. This time, the government wants to prevent such problems before they start.

Important Advice for Residents

Continue normal fuel and LPG usage.

Do not buy extra cylinders or fuel without need.

Use official websites and customer care numbers for updates.

Avoid forwarding unverified social media messages.

The Delhi government has repeated that there is no crisis in fuel supply. Citizens are being asked to remain calm and trust the existing supply system. With proper coordination between oil companies, transport officials, and distributors, normal services are expected to continue across the city.
