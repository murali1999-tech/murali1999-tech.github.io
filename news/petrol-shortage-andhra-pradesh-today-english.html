Today, a noticeable petrol and diesel shortage was observed in several parts of Andhra Pradesh. In cities like Vijayawada, Guntur, Rajahmundry, Tirupati, and Kurnool, people were seen standing in long queues at petrol stations waiting to refuel their vehicles.

Situation in Vijayawada

In Vijayawada, areas such as Benz Circle, Auto Nagar, and Patamata faced severe shortages. Many petrol bunks ran out of stock completely. Some fuel station owners said that supply might arrive by night. However, due to heavy rush since afternoon, the shortage became more visible.

Issues in Guntur and Rajahmundry

In Guntur, areas like Brodipet and Ambedkar Nagar also experienced similar conditions. In Rajahmundry, near Godavari Pushkar Ghat, petrol was unavailable in several bunks, forcing people to travel to other areas.

Main Reasons for Shortage

Officials mentioned several key reasons behind this situation:

  • Delay in fuel transportation
  • Technical issues at fuel depots
  • Impact of worker strikes in some regions
  • Panic buying by the public

These factors together caused temporary disruption in supply.

Public Experiences

Ramesh from Vijayawada said, “I have been waiting since 7 AM. It took two hours to get petrol.”

Sunitha, an employee from Guntur, said, “I got late to office. Every petrol bunk had long queues.”

Government Response

The state government has reviewed the situation and is coordinating with oil companies. Officials clarified that steps are being taken to restore normal supply quickly and that this is only a temporary issue.

Diesel Situation

Diesel shortage was also reported in some areas. Truck and bus drivers faced difficulties, affecting transportation services due to delayed supply.

Advice to Public

Authorities have advised people:

  • Do not store fuel unnecessarily
  • Avoid panic buying
  • Check nearby fuel availability before visiting

Conclusion

Overall, the petrol and diesel shortage seen today in Andhra Pradesh is temporary. Officials assure that supply will return to normal soon. People are requested to stay calm and use fuel only when necessary.

This situation teaches an important lesson — during supply disruptions, awareness, patience, and cooperation are essential to overcome the issue smoothly.
