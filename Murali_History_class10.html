The Union Executive

ICSE Class 10 Civics – Premium Notes

The Union Executive is the central governing body responsible for implementing laws in India. It includes the President, Vice-President, Prime Minister, and the Council of Ministers, all of whom play important roles in administration.

The President of India is the constitutional head of the country. Although all executive powers are exercised in the President’s name, in reality, these powers are used on the advice of the Prime Minister and the Council of Ministers.

The President is elected indirectly by an Electoral College consisting of elected Members of Parliament and State Legislative Assemblies. The term of office is five years, and the President can be re-elected.

The President performs several important functions such as appointing the Prime Minister, summoning Parliament, giving assent to bills, and declaring emergencies. However, these powers are mostly formal in nature.

The Vice-President is the second highest authority in the country and acts as the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha. In the absence of the President, the Vice-President performs the duties of the President.

The Prime Minister is the real executive head of the country and holds the most important position in the government. He or she is the leader of the majority party in the Lok Sabha and is appointed by the President.

The Prime Minister forms the Council of Ministers and allocates portfolios to them. He or she also acts as a link between the President and the Council of Ministers and plays a key role in decision-making.

The Council of Ministers assists the President in the administration of the country. It consists of Cabinet Ministers, Ministers of State, and Deputy Ministers, and works under the leadership of the Prime Minister.

The Council of Ministers follows the principle of collective responsibility, which means all ministers are jointly responsible to the Lok Sabha for their decisions and actions.

The Cabinet is the most powerful group within the Council of Ministers. It consists of senior ministers who take major policy decisions and guide the functioning of the government.

In India’s parliamentary system, there is a clear distinction between the nominal executive (President) and the real executive (Prime Minister). This ensures a balance of power and effective governance.

The executive is closely connected with the legislature, and the government remains in power only as long as it has the support of the majority in the Lok Sabha.

The President also has the power to issue ordinances when Parliament is not in session and can grant pardons in judicial cases.

During times of emergency, the President can exercise special powers, which may even affect the normal functioning of the Constitution and Fundamental Rights.

Overall, the Union Executive ensures the smooth functioning of the government by making decisions, implementing laws, and maintaining administration across the country.

The Indian Executive

The Indian Executive refers to the branch of government responsible for implementing laws and running the administration of the country. It includes the President, Vice-President, Prime Minister, and the Council of Ministers.

The President

The President of India is the constitutional head of the country. All executive actions are carried out in the name of the President, but in practice, these powers are exercised on the advice of the Prime Minister and the Council of Ministers.

Election and Term of President

The President is elected indirectly by an Electoral College consisting of elected Members of Parliament and State Legislative Assemblies. The President holds office for a term of five years and can be re-elected.

Powers of the President

The President performs various functions such as appointing the Prime Minister, summoning Parliament, giving assent to bills, and declaring emergencies. The President also has the power to issue ordinances when Parliament is not in session.

The Vice-President

The Vice-President is the second highest authority in the country and serves as the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha. In case of the President’s absence or vacancy, the Vice-President performs the functions of the President.

The Prime Minister

The Prime Minister is the real executive head of the country and plays the most important role in governance. The Prime Minister is the leader of the majority party in the Lok Sabha and is appointed by the President.

Functions of the Prime Minister

The Prime Minister forms the Council of Ministers and allocates departments to them. He or she also coordinates the working of the government and takes major policy decisions.

The Council of Ministers

The Council of Ministers assists the President in the administration of the country. It consists of different categories of ministers and works under the leadership of the Prime Minister.

Collective Responsibility

The Council of Ministers follows the principle of collective responsibility, which means all ministers are jointly responsible to the Lok Sabha for their actions and decisions.

The Cabinet

The Cabinet is the inner core of the Council of Ministers and consists of senior ministers. It is the main decision-making body of the government and plays a crucial role in policy formulation.

Parliamentary System

India follows a parliamentary system of government where there is a distinction between the nominal executive (President) and the real executive (Prime Minister).

Executive and Legislature

The executive is closely linked with the legislature, and the government continues in power only as long as it enjoys the confidence of the majority in the Lok Sabha.

Judicial and Emergency Powers

The President also has judicial powers such as granting pardons and can exercise special powers during emergencies, which may affect the normal functioning of the Constitution.

Conclusion

Overall, the Indian Executive ensures effective governance by implementing laws, maintaining administration, and taking important decisions for the country.
