The Union Executive

The Union Executive is the central governing body responsible for implementing laws in India. It includes the President, Vice-President, Prime Minister, and the Council of Ministers.

The President

The President is the constitutional head of India. All executive actions are carried out in the President’s name but exercised on advice.

Prime Minister

The Prime Minister is the real executive head and leader of the majority party in the Lok Sabha. He forms and leads the government.

Council of Ministers

The Council of Ministers assists the President and works under the Prime Minister following collective responsibility.

The Cabinet

The Cabinet is the core decision-making body consisting of senior ministers.

Parliamentary System

India follows a system where the President is nominal head and Prime Minister is real executive.

Conclusion

The Union Executive ensures smooth governance by implementing laws and managing administration.
